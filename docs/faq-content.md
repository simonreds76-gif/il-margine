## SECTION 2: BETTING PHILOSOPHY & APPROACH

### Q: What does "betting with mathematical edge" mean?

**A:** Edge is the difference between true probability and implied probability from bookmaker odds.

**Example:**
- True probability of Saka getting 2+ shots on target: 55%
- Bookmaker odds: 2.10 (implies 47.6% probability)
- Edge: 55% - 47.6% = 7.4%

When you consistently bet with positive edge, you profit long-term despite short-term variance.

**Our edge sources:**
1. Former compiler experience identifying mispriced markets
2. Focus on markets with less sophisticated pricing models
3. Systematic analysis bookmakers don't prioritize
4. Bookmaker-to-bookmaker price discrepancies

Edge isn't about "feeling" or "expert opinion." It's mathematical probability assessment.

### Q: What are player props in football betting?

**A:** **Player props** (short for propositions) are bets on individual player statistics within a match, rather than the match outcome.

**Common player prop markets:**

**Why we focus here:** Bookmakers price these markets less efficiently than match odds, creating exploitable value.

### Q: What is the difference between "Over 1.5 shots" and "2+ shots"?

**A:** They're **functionally identical** but presented differently:

- **Over 1.5 shots** = Player needs 2 or more
- **2+ shots** = Player needs 2 or more
- **Under 1.5 shots** = Player needs 0 or 1

Some bookmakers use "Over/Under" terminology, others use "2+ / 1 or fewer."

**Key point:** "1.5" is the line. You can't score 1.5 shots, so it's binary:
- 2+ shots = Over wins
- 0-1 shots = Under wins

This applies to all fractional lines: Over 2.5 fouls = 3+ fouls needed.

---

### Q: What does "anytime goalscorer" (ATG) mean?

**A:** **Anytime Goalscorer** is a bet that a specific player will score at least one goal during the match, regardless of:
- When they score (1st minute or 90th minute)
- How many they score (1 goal or hat-trick both win)
- Match result (player can score in a losing team)

**Example:**
- Bet: Erling Haaland Anytime Goalscorer @ 1.80
- Result: Man City wins 3-1, Haaland scores 2 goals
- Outcome: Bet wins (only needed 1 goal)

**ATG vs First Goalscorer:**
- ATG: Player scores anytime in match
- First Goalscorer: Player scores the first goal of the match (higher odds)

We'll be launching ATG selections soon, focusing on opposition-specific scoring patterns and tactical matchups.

### Q: What are game handicaps in tennis?

**A:** **Game handicaps** are spread bets on the total games won by each player.

**Example:**
- Match: Alcaraz vs Tiafoe
- Handicap: Alcaraz -3.5 games @ 1.95

**How it settles:**
- Alcaraz wins 6-3, 6-4 (10 games vs 7 games)
- Game differential: 10 - 7 = 3 games
- Handicap was -3.5, so Alcaraz needed to win by 4+ games
- **Result: Bet loses** (won by 3, needed 4)

**Alternative outcome:**
- Alcaraz wins 6-2, 6-3 (12 games vs 5 games)
- Differential: 7 games
- **Result: Bet wins** (won by 7, needed 4)

**Why we bet these:** Match odds are efficient. Game handicaps are less efficient because bookmakers underweight surface-specific factors and matchup analysis.

---

### Q: What does "total games Over/Under" mean in tennis?

**A:** **Total games** is the combined number of games played by both players.

**Example:**
- Match: Sinner vs Rublev
- Total: Over 21.5 games @ 1.90

**How it settles:**
- Result: Sinner wins 7-6, 6-4
- Games: 7+6+6+4 = 23 games total
- **Result: Bet wins** (23 > 21.5)

**Alternative outcome:**
- Result: Sinner wins 6-3, 6-2
- Games: 6+3+6+2 = 17 games
- **Result: Bet loses** (17 < 21.5)

**Factors affecting totals:**
- Serve dominance (big servers = fewer break points = fewer games)
- Surface speed (clay = longer rallies = more breaks = more games)
- Player styles (defensive baseline grinders = more games)

We target totals when bookmakers misprice serve/return matchups or surface factors.
